Koppedu is a traditional sacred structure built as an offering during Amman festivals, symbolizing devotion and gratitude. Families or individuals construct the koppedu (usually a small hut or structure made of coconut leaves, bamboo, and cloth) and stay in it for a few days, following rituals and prayers with purity and dedication. It reflects deep faith, simplicity, and the cultural spirit of the festival.
